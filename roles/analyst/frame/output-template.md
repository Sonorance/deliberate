# Frame

## Problem

_Who is struggling and the jobs they're trying to get done — solution-free. One block per distinct
persona; keep persona lines short._

### _Persona 1_

_Who they are and their situation, in a short phrase or one crisp sentence — no biography._

* I want to _[job to be done]_, so I can _[outcome]_.
* I want to _[job to be done]_, so I can _[outcome]_.

_(Repeat the persona block for each distinct persona, including any adjacent persona materially affected —
noting how the problem touches them. No grounding, evidence, sources, or assumptions block.)_

## Validation

_Evidence that the problems above are **real**, not assumed — **quantitative** (telemetry, usage data,
survey results) or **qualitative** (verbatim field / sales / customer / partner quotes, each attributed to
the real person or company by name). **~3 concise bullets, 5 max.** Every figure and quote must be real and
traceable to the sources — **never invent a number, quote, company, or source**. If a problem can't be
grounded in real evidence, say so plainly (e.g. "no direct evidence yet — assumption") rather than
fabricating one._

* _a quantitative signal with its source (e.g. "38% of onboarding sessions drop at step 3 — product telemetry, Q2"), **or** a real attributed quote (e.g. "'We rebuild this by hand every release.' — Platform lead, Acme Corp") — grounded, never invented._

## Competitive landscape

_Whether today's alternatives already offer **what this case proposes — exactly as the Problem above
frames it** — and where the product can credibly differentiate on that specific change._

_The defensible gap on **this specific change** in **one strong bullet — two at most**. If there is no
real differentiation because it's **table-stakes** everyone already offers, **say so explicitly** — that
