# product & experience case

Use this lens when the primary commitment is to create, change, simplify, or remove a customer-facing capability or experience. The decision is about what users should be able to do and how the product should enable it.

## Frame

Define the solution-free problem through grounded personas, jobs-to-be-done, evidence, current alternatives, and the product-specific gap. Keep persona descriptions short and let their jobs carry the substance. Do not let a proposed feature substitute for evidence of a real job or outcome.

## Shape

Shape the simplest coherent concept that moves the primary persona through the job on the product's real primary surface. Make the hero journey concrete and surface-native, cover other personas only where the flow materially differs, and draw explicit scope boundaries.

## Launch

Sequence only the shaped concept: a hero MVP that validates the customer need, necessary fast-follows, and later breadth. Name the first reachable users, the value proposition, any credible compounding loop, and telemetry-derived success and guardrail metrics.

## Evaluation

A product case must have grounded demand, a reachable user, strategic and trust fit, and a concept that credibly resolves the framed job. Differentiation, compounding advantage, delight, agent-readiness, and openness can raise a passing case; lacking a standalone moat must not sink a necessary enabler or parity move.

## One-pager

Use a reverse PR-FAQ in the representative customer's first-person voice. Distil the before-and-after, initial experience, outcomes, honest boundaries, alternatives, and why now without inventing a testimonial or capability.

## Prototype

Prototype the primary journey end-to-end in the native medium of each primary product surface. Mock data and backends, but reuse the product's real visual, command, API, agent-tool, or physical conventions.
