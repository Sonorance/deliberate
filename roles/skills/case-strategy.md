# strategy & portfolio case

Use this lens when the primary commitment is where to play, how to win, what to prioritize, sequence, fund, or stop, or which capability the organization must build. The decision should change resource allocation or constrain future choices; a slogan is not a strategy.

## Frame

State the decision, triggering change, diagnosis, objective, constraints, stakeholders, evidence, and cost of maintaining the current direction. Distinguish a durable strategic issue from a temporary operational problem.

## Shape

Develop mutually distinct choices, including the status quo where credible. Compare fit with the objective, advantage, opportunity cost, required capabilities, dependencies, reversibility, and trust implications. Recommend one choice and make its commitments and explicit non-choices concrete.

## Launch

Translate the choice into sequencing, resource or capability commitments, immediate decisions, leading indicators, guardrails, and revisit triggers. Do not turn the section into a feature roadmap or claim precision the evidence cannot support.

## Evaluation

A strategy case must have a grounded diagnosis, a meaningful choice, coherence with the project's objective, a credible source of advantage or improved position, and commitments the organization can actually make. Penalize option lists that avoid tradeoffs, recommendations with no resource implications, and directions whose success cannot be observed or revisited.

## One-pager

Write an executive strategy memo: diagnosis, strategic choice, why this option, commitments, non-choices, sequence, indicators, risks, and the questions a skeptical decision-maker will ask.
