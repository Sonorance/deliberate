# platform & ecosystem case

Use this lens when the primary commitment is to build, buy, adopt, integrate, migrate, partner, standardize, or depend on a platform, protocol, provider, or ecosystem player. The decision is about control, leverage, interoperability, and dependency—not merely implementation detail.

## Frame

State the platform or ecosystem decision, triggering change, affected users and systems, current architecture or relationship, evidence, hard constraints, dependency exposure, and cost of doing nothing. Ordinary library selection belongs here only when it creates a material product or business commitment.

## Shape

Compare a small set of viable options, including the current approach where credible, across user and product value, interoperability, control, lock-in, reversibility, security and trust, operational burden, migration cost, partner incentives, and standards fit. Recommend one target relationship or architecture and draw its boundaries.

## Launch

Sequence technical and organizational validation, proof points, migration or adoption, rollback, compatibility, ownership, and measurable success. Surface external dependencies and decision checkpoints rather than hiding them behind an optimistic implementation plan.

## Evaluation

A platform case must create grounded user or strategic value, fit hard constraints, and offer a credible adoption or migration path. Evaluate control versus leverage, interoperability, reversibility, trust, total operational exposure, and ecosystem durability. A fashionable standard or partner name is not evidence by itself.

## One-pager

Write a platform decision memo: current exposure, options, chosen approach, value, tradeoffs, architecture or relationship boundary, migration and rollback, dependencies, indicators, and unresolved risks.
