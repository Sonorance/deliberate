# Case evaluation

Score the completed case as an advisory second opinion on a 0–10 scale. The score answers whether the recommended decision should advance, not whether the document is polished.

## Method

1. Apply the case lens's necessary criteria first. If a critical condition is essentially absent, a hard constraint or non-goal is violated, or the recommendation creates unacceptable trust risk, reject; no secondary strength can compensate.
2. For a case that clears those conditions, let its weakest necessary condition set the floor, then lift only for evidence-backed strengths specific to that lens.
3. Treat evidence quality as a confidence cap. Unsupported demand, economics, reach, advantage, feasibility, partner intent, or technical claims cannot receive the benefit of the doubt.
4. Judge the meaningful alternatives and the status quo, not the recommendation in isolation. Name why this choice is preferable now.

## Verdicts

- **7.0–10 · advance** — the decision is well grounded, coherent, and ready for the proposed commitment or validation.
- **5.0–6.9 · shelve** — potentially valuable but a material uncertainty or missing condition blocks commitment; identify the evidence or change that would move it.
- **0–4.9 · reject** — a necessary condition fails, the recommendation is off-strategy or trust-unsafe, or a credible alternative clearly dominates.

Scores are meaningful within the lens and case; do not imply that a Product score and a Strategy score are directly rankable.

## Output

Publish only the score and verdict, one-line why now, and the one to three points that actually drive the result. Keep the internal criteria and math out of the deliverable.
