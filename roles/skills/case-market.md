# market & commercial case

Use this lens when the primary commitment is who to serve and how to position, package, price, distribute, launch, or sell the product. The decision is about market choice and the commercial motion, not a disguised feature request.

## Frame

State the market decision, triggering signal, affected segment or buyer, current perception or commercial motion, alternatives, and grounded evidence. Separate observed behavior from assumptions; never invent willingness to pay, channel access, conversion, or buyer urgency.

## Shape

Develop a small set of real alternatives, including keeping the current motion. Compare target segment, alternative frame, category, differentiated value, proof, offer or packaging, and channel implications as relevant. Recommend one coherent market shape and name who or what it deliberately excludes.

## Launch

Turn the recommendation into falsifiable message, offer, pricing, packaging, or channel tests. Sequence the smallest credible validation before broad rollout, define evidence thresholds and guardrails, and state what would cause the team to scale, revise, or stop.

## Evaluation

A market case must rest on a grounded target need, a credible and reachable buyer or user, differentiated value the product can prove, strategic coherence, and a testable commercial path. Score unsupported willingness to pay or channel reach as uncertainty, not fact. Strong positioning cannot compensate for an audience the project cannot reach or a promise the product cannot substantiate.

## One-pager

Write an internal market decision brief: chosen audience, current alternative or perception, recommended positioning or commercial motion, proof, exclusions, validation, and unresolved questions. It is decision material, not outbound copy.

## Prototype

Prototype the selected market hypothesis at the customer touchpoint where it can be judged: for example a landing page, pricing or packaging page, campaign/message sequence, sales narrative, or partner-facing offer. It must expose the claim, proof, action, and test signal; it is not automatically a product UI.
