# Repository guidance for AI agents

This repo implements **Deliberate** — a local, files-first product toolkit for product managers, founders, business development, marketing, and adjacent roles. It ships primarily as a **`/deliberate` skill** for agent harnesses; the host produces cases, briefs, product readouts, matchups, context, prototypes, and reviewable records in-session. Sonorance is the stable app/platform and agent-review workbench Deliberate runs on; Deliberate contributes capabilities additively and never replaces or rebrands it.

## Golden rules
- **Ground every claim.** Each conclusion must trace to the case, the attached project-external sources, the three project-context files (`product.md`, `competitors.md`, `ecosystem.md`), or an explicit assumption labelled as such. Never invent demand, personas, competitors, or distribution channels.
- **Stay in scope.** Use the **skills** (reusable method/craft) and the **project context** (from `deliberate/context/product.md`) injected into your prompt before producing anything. Respect the project's non-goals and objective.
- **Append, don't overwrite.** Each stage appends its artifact to the case's accumulated context (the running record of everything prior). Do not contradict earlier stages without flagging it explicitly.
- **Be concise and structured.** Output the exact sections your stage's instruction file specifies — nothing more.
- **Fill the template; never echo its instructions.** Output templates describe each section with _italic guidance between underscores_ and parentheticals — these are instructions **to you**, not content. Replace every one with your real, grounded content: keep the section **headings** and fill in the tables, but **never reproduce the italic guidance, placeholder, or any `_…_` prompt text in your output**, and don't add a grounding / assumptions / notes section the template didn't ask for.
- **Outputs are end-user deliverables — never talk about the run.** The artifact is a finished document a customer reads. **Never** include commentary about your own execution, the task/instructions, the pipeline, tools, sessions, or previous responses (e.g. "the artifact was already produced in my previous response", "the task was to…", "as an AI…", "no further action needed"). Output only the deliverable content itself.
- **Do your stage's job objectively.** Each role analyzes thoroughly and without prejudging worth — the Analyst frames the problem and designs the concept + its go-to-market; the Evaluator scores. **Only Score (the Evaluator) scores go/no-go.** Don't kill, shelve, or hedge a case because it might be weak; that decision isn't yours.
- **Curate durable signal, not snapshots or inventories.** Init reads relevant files inside the current project as automatic context; `.sonorance/sources.md` is only for resources outside that folder, whether internal/private or public. Prefer systems and corpora that accumulate many signals over time—document folders, repositories, dashboards/queries, CRM or support systems, issue trackers, research repositories—and durable canonical documents. Never register an isolated feedback file, one support case, a point-in-time export, or another small observation as project context. Competitor and ecosystem discovery maximizes qualified coverage through independent discovery lanes and saturation passes, then admits only named entities with current relationship/overlap evidence, material relevance, and official monitoring sources. Competitors exclude manual/status-quo substitutes. Ecosystem complements are distinct co-used products/services whose interoperability raises mutual value in a shared workflow—not substitutes, dependency-only vendors, or generic tools used by the same person.
- **Hand off with a concrete CTA.** Every substantive workflow ends by explaining the most valuable next action and asking for it through the harness's native confirmation UI: init → brief; brief/readout/matchup → open the result in Sonorance and run recommended cases concurrently by default, open it only, or another action; product/market case → prototype; strategy/platform case → record review; prototype/address → review.

## Roles, stages & skills (how they're tied together)
Each part's method lives under `roles/<role>/<part>/instructions.md` plus lens-specific output templates and reusable skills in `roles/skills/`. A case records one primary decision lens: **product & experience**, **market & commercial**, **strategy & portfolio**, or **platform & ecosystem**. The user-facing command stays `/deliberate case <idea>`; the host infers the primary commitment, asks only when genuinely ambiguous, and passes the lens internally when it creates the case. The **Analyst** writes the complete `frame → shape → launch` funnel through that lens: Frame establishes the unresolved decision and evidence, Shape compares alternatives and recommends a direction, and Launch turns it into clear next commitments. The **Evaluator** judges the completed recommendation through lens-specific criteria, preferably as an isolated cross-vendor sub-agent. The **Prototyper** builds a source-grounded product journey or market test artifact on request: product prototypes make every shaped step and a failure/recovery path reachable in the native medium, while market prototypes expose one grounded hypothesis through a real customer touchpoint and observable response. No prototype is available for strategy or platform cases. Score and eligible prototypes are recomputable companions, not funnel stages.

**Beyond the per-case funnel, a fourth role — the Briefer (`brief`)** — produces a project-scoped **landscape brief** (`/deliberate brief [period]`): the competitive + market changes for one selected period. The first run defaults to the previous 90 days; later runs continue from the previous brief with the same cap unless the user supplies a natural-language override. It is **not a case** and not a funnel stage (no score, no prototype): one host-run pass writes a single Markdown report to `deliberate/briefs/<YYYY-MM-DD>/brief.md`, applying the `landscape-scan` skill (timeframe discipline, mandatory source links, hard filtering to executive-worthy signal, then Action items for the project). Its method lives under `roles/briefer/brief/` and is bound like the host-run parts (no model). Never re-report a change a prior brief already covered; ground every highlight in a dated source inside the window.

**A fifth role — the Reporter (`readout`)** — produces a project-scoped **product readout** (`/deliberate readout [period]`): the key metrics, customer evidence, material insights, and warranted actions from the project's configured sources. One completed reporting period grounds the entire report; the default is the previous completed Monday–Sunday calendar week under the cadence/alignment/timezone stored in `product.md`, and a user may override one run with a completed period such as June or Q2. Every metric uses that report-level period and the immediately preceding equivalent completed period, so the table does not repeat dates per row. Data from the period currently in progress is excluded rather than projected or compared as complete. When a decision-relevant metric has enough comparable history, the Reporter may add one of up to three deterministic SVG trend charts under `charts/`; charts supplement the exact table values and never smooth, interpolate, forecast, or display targets. It is **not a case** and not a funnel stage: each host-run pass creates `deliberate/readouts/<YYYY-MM-DD[-N]>/readout.md`, keeping same-day runs as separate time-series artifacts. Its method lives under `roles/reporter/readout/`. Distinguish observations, context, hypotheses, and causal evidence; use exact representative feedback quotes only when source/date/prevalence and privacy-safe attribution are available; surface unavailable or contradictory evidence under Data gaps rather than filling it in.

**A sixth role — the Scout (`matchup`)** — produces a project-scoped, single-competitor **head-to-head** (`/deliberate matchup <competitor>`): a grounded teardown of us vs. one named rival across gaps, strengths/weaknesses, UX & user journey, supported scenarios, positioning, target JTBD, and partnership/reuse opportunities — deck-ready. Unlike the date-keyed brief, a matchup is **competitor-keyed and refresh-in-place**: one host-run pass writes a single Markdown report to `deliberate/matchups/<competitor-slug>/matchup.md`, and re-running it for the same competitor **overwrites in place** (keeping the id + created-at, updating the `as_of` timestamp). It carries an `as_of` in frontmatter **and** an "As of <date>." line in the body (so a point-in-time read survives even when metadata isn't rendered). It is **not a case** and not a funnel stage (no score, no prototype), applying the `head-to-head` skill (comparison framework, mandatory source links, executive-worthy signal). Its method lives under `roles/scout/matchup/` and is bound like the host-run parts (no model). Ground every claim in a dated source; keep it to one rival per report.

**Each stage's method is bound in `roles/config.yaml`, not loaded globally.** Lensed case stages map a shared instruction file to a template and supporting skill bundle per lens; non-case roles keep one default binding. The engine reads and validates the whole matrix on every invocation and injects only the selected files. Missing lenses, malformed YAML, unknown stages, incomplete bindings, unsupported prototype lenses, and missing referenced files are hard failures; never add a silent fallback that can produce a plausible but methodologically wrong prompt. The `score` Evaluator carries the common case-evaluation and critique methods plus the selected lens criteria; isolation/provenance comes from how the harness actually runs and saves it, not from configuration alone.

**Auxiliary work is done by the HOST, not engine agents.** There are no `title`, `describe`, or `contextualize` model agents anymore. The **project context** lives as three host-written Markdown files: `deliberate/context/product.md` owns overview, positioning, personas, jobs, interfaces, market, commercial context, customer voice, metrics, strategy, objective, and non-goals; its Competitors and Ecosystem sections contain links only. `competitors.md` is the single source of truth for the competitor roster, details, and monitoring sources; `ecosystem.md` is the single source of truth for the ecosystem roster, details, and monitoring sources. Every downstream prompt receives all three files. Init idempotently adds a root README section linking `deliberate/context/` and telling agent harnesses to read it before product, strategy, market, positioning, or implementation work. Metrics & traction owns the shared readout cadence/timezone and durable metric semantics rather than mutable values. The host never fabricates a section it can't ground. During `/deliberate init`, every relevant file inside the project is read directly as automatic grounding and is never proposed or persisted in `.sonorance/sources.md`. Project-external source discovery is section-led across product/strategy, code/delivery, metrics/data, customer voice, and go-to-market; it prefers durable systems, corpora, and canonical documents with recurring signal, accepts fewer—or none—when sufficient, and never pads with isolated observations. Every discovered candidate is shown as `- <location> - <description>` before approval. Public product-owned GitHub repositories contribute their enabled Issues pages to Customer voice. Competitor discovery covers each commercial lane and ecosystem discovery covers each strategic position through independent routes until a saturation pass yields no new qualified entity; final rosters have no numeric cap but every entry must pass the evidence gate. The **case title** is likewise the host's job.

Case references may use a full id or a unique prefix. Never guess when a prefix matches multiple cases: the CLI rejects ambiguity so an agent cannot persist output into an arbitrary record. Project-required commands resolve only the initialized current working directory; they must fail rather than mutate another globally selected vault. Likewise, comment bridge transport/server errors are command failures, never success-shaped empty queues.

Every case operation after creation carries the printed full id or a unique prefix; there is no singleton active case because several cases can progress concurrently. Score provenance must be literal, not aspirational. The model printed by `case score prompt <case-id>` is the target; `case score save <case-id>` receives the actual evaluator model. Pass `--independent` only when an isolated evaluator produced the artifact. If the harness must score inline, omit `--independent`; `score.md` will visibly label the same-session fallback and telemetry will record the content-free guardrail.

## Human gates
The pipeline stops after the analysis (`launch`). For product and market cases, Prototype does not auto-start: ask for the lens-appropriate prototype and build only on approval or an explicit `/deliberate case <id> prototype`. Prototype is unavailable for strategy and platform cases; offer to open their completed decision record for review instead.

## Execution model (host-driven; the engine runs no model)
Every stage is driven by the agent **harness**, not the engine: infer the lens → create the case with that internal lens value → `prompt <stage>` → the host produces the artifact → `save <stage>`. The engine is **LLM-free** and never classifies prompts itself; there is no headless run, background scheduler, or auto-resume. Keep eligible Prototype asks human-gated and never add engine-side auto-advance. Sonorance is the existing local app for reviewing and addressing the resulting files; it does not run the workflow.

## State & memory
A run's records live in the initialized current repo as **plain Markdown/JSON — no database**. Its `deliberate/` folder holds `context/` (`product.md` + `competitors.md` + `ecosystem.md`) plus `cases/<YYYY-MM-DD-slug>/`. A case is just a prompt, not its own file; the durable combined record is **`analysis.md`** with a frontmatter `lens`, title, summary, Key highlights, and one clean lens-shaped section per stage. Existing records without `lens` default to Product for compatibility. Eligible product/market prototypes are `prototype/[<surface>/]index.html` sidecars; **`score.md`** records evaluator provenance; **`one-pager.md`** uses the selected lens's one-page format; `log.jsonl` holds the run log. Within a run, each stage sees the accumulated case context plus all three read-only project-context files.
